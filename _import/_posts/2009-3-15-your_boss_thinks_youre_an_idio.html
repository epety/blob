--- layout: post title: Your Boss Thinks You're an Idiot mt_id: 72 ---
If you're a good programmer, chances are that somebody higher up in the food chain at your company thinks you're a moron.  That person is probably an MBA type who doesn't understand anything about computers, but still has the power to fire you.

Yeah, it's a bitch, but take it as a complement.

This person first thought you were an idiot when you were in a meeting with him or her, discussing some esoteric technical detail of a project that this person has a stake in.  Maybe it was a programming language choice, data interchange format decision, or algorithm discussion.  Whatever it was, Mr. MBA latched on like a hyena, because it had been thirty minutes and he hadn't yet spoken.  When someone first started to discuss using Python for a new project, and Mr. MBA heard a project manager say that Python is a more productive language than Java, his inner Alan Turing came out.

"I think that if we can get to market faster with Python than with Java, then it's a big win, what do you think, Ted?"

Uhh, deer in the headlights.  Is the Global Interpreter Lock going to burn us anywhere?  Are there adequate APIs for our databases?  How much time are we going to spend figuring out the build system when most of the team is at least familiar with Ant?

I'm stalling with "uhhh", trying to evaluate edge cases that could sink the project, and Mr. MBA is already figuring out how he's going to take the credit for choosing Python.  The best I can come up with is "Well, I don't know, there are tradeoffs".  Not exactly the cogent, expert opinion I was hoping for.
